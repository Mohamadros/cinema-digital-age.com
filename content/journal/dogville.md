Dogville is not a film I would recommend to every moviegoer.

> “Evil can arise anywhere, if the conditions are right.”
>
> — Lars von Trier

Some critics have even described Dogville as inhuman. After watching it, you will likely find yourself caught between admiration and discomfort. You may hate it, you may love it—but you will not forget it.

Despite its three-hour runtime, the film never feels long. Time slips away almost unnoticed, and by the end you are left reflecting far more than you expected.

Is Dogville a portrait of human suffering? A story about the loss of humanity in the face of collective power? A philosophical tragedy? A modern Christian allegory? Or perhaps something deeply personal from Lars von Trier himself?

There are no easy answers. Dogville is a film that challenges, provokes, and stays with you long after the credits roll.
